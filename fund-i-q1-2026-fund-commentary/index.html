Portfolio Highlights

The three positions below together represent the majority of Fund I's residual NAV and, in our view, the most meaningful drivers of DPI from here. Each is executing against its plan in a way that warrants explicit flagging this quarter — not because of a discrete event, but because the trajectories are setting up the exit paths we have underwritten.

Popmenu

Entry: Series A, October 2019 · Headquarters: Atlanta, GA · Base10 ownership: 17.8%

Popmenu is Fund I's largest position and the clearest example in the book of a Business-in-a-Box company compounding into a real platform. The front-office operating system for independent and small-group restaurants now serves 10,000+ locations at ~$50M ARR — up from ~$1M ARR at our 2019 entry — with Q4 2025 marking its first EBITDA-positive quarter and a credible path to cash-flow positivity by year-end 2026. Management is targeting ~$75M ARR this year through organic growth plus an active ~$20M M&A pipeline. Strategic interest is already live: Toast has approached twice, and the SevenRooms-to-DoorDash precedent ($1.2B sale at ~11x ARR in May 2025) frames the obvious comp. This is the single position most likely to set Fund I's terminal DPI.

WeTravel

Entry: Series A, May 2019 · Headquarters: San Francisco, CA · Base10 ownership: 11.9%

WeTravel is the operating system and payment network for multi-day travel businesses — tour operators, retreat organizers, educational and specialty agencies — processing $1.3B in gross transaction volume in 2025 at $55M+ ARR (+63% YoY). In August 2025 the company closed its Series C at a $432M post-money led by Sapphire Ventures, a competitive round with seven term sheets that validated the take-rate expansion thesis (1.86% in 2025, tracking toward 3.9% by 2028). Under Ted Clements (who built FareHarbor's GTM before its $250M sale to Booking.com), WeTravel has consistently beaten plan and ended 2025 near breakeven with $44.8M in cash. Exit path is IPO (2028–2029) or strategic acquisition by an OTA.

Shelf

Entry: Series A, March 2020 · Headquarters: Stamford, CT · Base10 ownership: 14.1%

Shelf is the position where the strategic reframe over the last year is most worth flagging. In H2 2025, management deprioritized the legacy knowledge management business and concentrated on Cortex — the company's knowledge graph and reasoning engine — as a full end-to-end agentic platform competing with Sierra, Decagon, and Wonderful in enterprise customer experience. The bet is that knowledge-graph-enhanced retrieval outperforms standard vector RAG in accuracy and hallucination, and early head-to-head wins (including displacing Wonderful as Nespresso's provider in Greece) support the thesis. The company ended 2025 at $18.5M CARR with 111% NDR and is targeting three signed agentic deals in Q1 plus ~10 more in Q2. The strategic buyer universe is deep (Salesforce, Microsoft, ServiceNow, Databricks, Snowflake); the next two quarters will determine whether Shelf clears the threshold for a meaningful Fund I outcome.

Fund I Outlook

Fund I is in its harvesting phase. Our focus across the remainder of 2026 is working with founders and management teams on exit readiness — particularly in the three positions above, which together drive the majority of residual NAV. We will update LPs on in-progress processes as they reach a stage where advance notice is appropriate. No write-downs or impairments were taken in Q1.
